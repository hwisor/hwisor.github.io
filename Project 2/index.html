Body of a Courtesan in Nine Stages of Decomposition

"Body of a Courtesan in Nine Stages" is an example of kusozu, a visual depiction of a corpse in the process of decay and decomposition. The handscroll was painted by Japanese artist Kobayashi Eitaku in the 17th century.

The kusozu, "painting of the nine stages" is derived from a traditional Buddhist doctrine that urges meditation on the temporary nature of life and the physical world by contemplating a decaying corpse.
